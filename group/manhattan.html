Spotlight Restaurants in Manhattan

Manhattan is the main borough in New York City where food and entertainment is on every street. We pick three food heaven neighborhoods in Manhattan:

  • Koreatown
  • Nolita
  • Hell's Kitchen

Koreatown

Koreatown is located at west 32nd street between 5th and 6th avenue. It was established in 1980s with a few Korean bookstore and restaurants. The area surround Koreatown is jammed with tourist because the nearby Empire State Building. It became convenient location for Korean imigrants to settle.

Nowadays, there are over 100 small businesses including restaurants, bars, karaoke, clubs, spas, bakeries and supermarket. This area is popular amongst clubbers and night owls because many restaurants are open 24/7.

  1. Woorijip

    2. Woorijip is a buffet-style restaurant that serve hot and cold Korean food. The portion is priced based on its weight per pound. It is inexpensive and perfect for a quick lunch.

    The Food Buffet
  2. Miss Korea BBQ

    3. Miss Korea BBQ is a three stories restaurant and a great place for group dining. Korean BBQ is a unique way of dining where the grill is mounted on the table. The patrons have the option to grill by themselves or have the waiters to serve them. The sizzling grilled meat can be wrapped in lettuce together with the sides for a mixture flavour.

    Korean BBQ with Side Dishes
  3. BCD Tofu House

    4. BCD Tofu House is famous for its tofu soup. The soup is served on a hot claypot bowl. The hot bowl causes the soup to stay boiling and dinners can cook an egg in the soup.

    Soon Tofu Soup

Nolita

Nolita is one of the neighborhood in downtown Manhattan near Soho, Little Italy, Chinatown, and Lower East Side. The word Nolita is derived from North of Little Italy.

San Gennero Feast is the annual event that take place in Nolita during September. It is a religious celebration with colorful parades, musical entertainment, and plenty of food and beverage stands. The celebration is along Mulberry Street from Grand Street to Houston Street.

  1. Cafe Habana

    2. Cafe Habana serves Cuban and Mexican cuisines. The space in this restaurant is very limited. But, it doesn't stop people from waiting in line for Habana's grilled corn Mexican style.

    Grilled Corn Topped with Cheese and Pepper Powder
  2. Lombardi's

    3. Lombardi's is a landmark pizzeria in Nolita. It was opened in 1905 by Gennaro Lombardi and it's been running for three generation. The pizza pies is baked in a tradition coal oven to preserve the authenticity.

    Margherita Pizza
  3. Rice to Riches

    4. Variety of flavourful rice pudding from sweet chocolate to tangy fruity flavour.

    Rice Pudding in Variety Flavour

Hell's Kitchen

Hell's Kitchen is located in Midtown Manhattan. It used to be a residence for working class Irish Ammerican. The neighborhood has been changing rapidly since 1970 through the architecture, businesses and restaurants.

Today, The Ninth Avenue International Food Festival is an annual event that every New Yorkers should experience. Food, drink and gift stands are filling the 9th avenue from 42nd street to 57th street on the third week of May.

  1. Per Se

    2. Per Se is an elite French restaurant overlooking the Central Park. In 2014, the Foxnews nominated Per Se as the third most expensive restaurant in the world that cost $851 per person.

    Sea Scallop and Foie Gras with Pomegranate Vinaigrette
  2. Ippudo Westside

    3. Ippudo is a crowded Japanese ramen restaurant. The average waiting time to dine is 45 minutes. Many people are willing to wait for a bowl of tasty homemade noodle soaked in Ippudo signature broth and topped with the choice of meats and seafood.

    Miso Ramen with Pork Loin Chashu
  3. Ousia

    4. Ousia is a Mediterranean Restaurant that located in a pyramid shaped building near the Hudson River.

    Grilled Octopus
