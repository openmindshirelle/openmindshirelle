Depressed in the City:

One in five New Yorkers have a mental health disorder, and at least 8 percent suffer from symptoms of depression, a new report by the city Department of Health says. Another report was conducted, results show that: Over half a million adult New Yorkers are estimated to have depression, yet less than percent report receiving care for it.

Quick Facts

-In NYC more than half of all premature deaths before age 75 are due to heart disease cancer, and diabetes
-Approximately one in nine adults in New Yorkers report having diabetes, and approximately one in four are obese, and another third are overweight.
-At least 60 percent of health is determined by social, environmental and behavioral factors; the remaining is due to genetics and the health care system

Interventions for Healthy Eating and Active Urban Living:

YMCA of Greater New York

The Diabetes Prevention Program, which is offered at YMCAs across the country including in NYC, is an evidence-based program that has demonstrated success in preventing the development of diabetes in high risk individuals.

Go!Kids Health and Fitness Programs

The Children's Aid Society Go Kids program teaches young children about nutrition and the fun in exploring new foods, as well as healthy body development and body image.
